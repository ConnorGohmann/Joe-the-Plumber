Common Questions (Q&A)

  • 1. How can I prevent my pipes from freezing?
  • 2. If my pipes burst, what should I do to stop the flooding?
  • 3. My washing machine smells like a sewer, What can I do?
  • 4. What size air conditioner do I need?
  • 5. My hot water keeps running out. Do I need a larger water heater?

Answers

  • A1. Begin by insulating the pipes that are located on the outside walls. And, if you have had previous problems with freezing, leave a faucet trickling warm water.
  • A2. In the event a pipe bursts, turn off the closest shut-off valve or your main water shut-off valve until a professional can arrive.
  • A3. Front loading washers sometimes develop mold around the thick gasket that seals the front door. Inspection the gasket itself and use bleach to disinfect
  • A4. A load calculation must be performed on your home to determine the proper system size that meets all the physical requirments. Ultimately, the size determination should be made by a qualified air conditioning specialist.
  • A5. There are many reasosns why you may be running out of hot water. Before insalling a larger heater, one of our technicans can analyze your options.
